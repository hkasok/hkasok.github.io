Harshin Kamal Asok

Ph.D. Candidate in Civil & Environmental Engineering
Florida International University (FIU), Miami, USA

I am a doctoral researcher focusing on urban microclimate modeling and nature-based climate adaptation. My work integrates computational fluid dynamics (CFD), urban energy balance modeling, and machine learning to simulate and mitigate the Urban Heat Island (UHI) effect in tropical cities.

My current research explores how urban trees, green roofs, and reflective materials influence near-ground temperature, humidity, and comfort conditions. Using advanced CFD models coupled with solar radiation and surface-energy balance formulations, I aim to bridge the gap between field observations, data-driven analytics, and physics-based simulations. This research supports urban climate resilience and sustainable planning in cities like Miami.
