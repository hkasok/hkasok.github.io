Research Overview

My research integrates computational fluid dynamics (CFD), urban energy balance modeling, and data-driven methods to understand and mitigate the Urban Heat Island (UHI) effect in tropical cities. The goal is to develop efficient numerical frameworks that can predict microclimatic conditions and evaluate nature-based cooling strategies such as tree canopies, green roofs, and reflective materials.

Urban Heat Island Mitigation

Urban Heat Island (UHI) effects occur when cities trap and store heat due to dense infrastructure and limited vegetation. My research investigates how surface materials, urban geometry, and vegetation alter energy exchange between air, buildings, and the ground. I model mitigation strategies such as tree canopy shading, evapotranspiration, and cool pavements to improve outdoor thermal comfort and urban sustainability.

CFD Simulation of Tree Canopies and Microclimate

Using OpenFOAM, I develop and apply CFD models that capture the detailed interactions between air flow, vegetation, and solar radiation. This includes modeling aerodynamic drag, leaf area density, and radiation exchange through the canopy using the Finite Volume Discrete Ordinates Method (fvDOM). The simulations are calibrated and validated with on-site field data collected under tree canopies in Miami.

Solar Radiation and Energy Balance Modeling

A major component of my research focuses on coupling radiative and conductive heat transfer processes using custom OpenFOAM solvers. I implement the Sky View Factor (SVF) and solar load models to represent the complex interaction of buildings and vegetation with sunlight and longwave radiation. This allows accurate prediction of surface and air temperature dynamics in heterogeneous urban environments.

Machine Learning for Urban Microclimate Prediction

I apply machine learning and physics-informed neural networks (PINNs) to predict urban temperature, humidity, and heat flux patterns using large-scale environmental and CFD-generated datasets. These models integrate physical constraints and data-driven learning to enable efficient prediction of microclimatic variables across space and time, providing a fast alternative to high-resolution CFD simulations.

Current and Future Work

  • Development of an immersed boundary CFD solver for urban energy simulations.
  • Coupling field sensor data with OpenFOAM simulations for model validation.
  • Exploring urban form optimization using AI-based surrogate models.
  • Collaborating with city planners to evaluate green infrastructure performance.
